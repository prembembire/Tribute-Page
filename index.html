FATHER OF ISRO

Vikram Sarabhai the renowned Indian scientist and visionary, holds a prominent place in India's scientific and technological history. Born on August 12, 1919, in Ahmedabad, he pursued his higher education at Cambridge University, earning his Ph.D. in Cosmic Rays in 1947. A trailblazer in space research, Sarabhai founded the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) in 1962, which later evolved into the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) in 1969. Under his leadership, India achieved its first satellite launch, Aryabhata, in 1975, and he established the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS) in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, as a significant center for space research and satellite launching activities. Besides his contributions to space exploration, Sarabhai played a crucial role in India's nuclear power program, being instrumental in the establishment of the Atomic Energy Commission of India and various nuclear research facilities. His legacy extends to the founding of prestigious institutions like the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL) and the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Ahmedabad.Vikram Sarabhai's exceptional achievements earned him several accolades, including the Padma Bhushan in 1966, and he remains celebrated as a pioneer of the Indian space program and a driving force behind India's scientific advancements on the international stage

Biography

  • Supported India's nuclear energy sector and established the Atomic Energy Commission of India.
  • Founded the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL) and the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Ahmedabad.
  • Received the Padma Bhushan award in 1966 for his contributions to science.
  • Passed away on December 30, 1971, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire scientists and researchers.
